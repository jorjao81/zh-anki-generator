Etymology

Origin: Phono-semantic compound (形声字)

唇chúnis a phono-semantic character analyzed as口 (semantic) + 辰 (phonetic). The component口kǒu"mouth" indicates the semantic field (a part associated with the mouth), while辰chénprovides the sound.

Historical notes: Early and seal forms commonly appear as脣(肉"flesh" +辰phonetic), emphasizing the lip as a body part. The received standard form is唇with the semantic indicator口. Both are 形声 with辰as the phonetic.

Classical sources: 《說文解字》 glosses the word as "the fleshy part at the border of the mouth," analyzing it as a 形声字 with辰as phonetic; the 《康熙字典》 records both脣and唇as interchangeable historical forms. The 漢語多功能字庫 analyzes the structure as⿱辰口(top辰, bottom口) in the modern graph.

The Semantic Evolution

"mouth/flesh" (semantic) + "辰" (phonetic)→ "the fleshy rim of the mouth" → modern meaning "lip(s)." Common derivatives include:嘴唇zuǐchún"lips,"唇膏chúngāo"lipstick," and the set phrase唇齒相依chúnchǐ xiāngyī"lips and teeth mutually depend."

Phonetic Series and Sound

辰functions as the phonetic in a family with similar onsets and related rimes:辰chén,晨chén,宸chén,振zhèn,娠shēn, and唇chún. The vowel variation (‑en/‑un) reflects historical sound change within the series.

Path:口/肉 (mouth, flesh) + 辰 (phonetic) → "fleshy edge of the mouth" → "lip(s)."
