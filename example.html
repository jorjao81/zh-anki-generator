Origin: Pictograph (象形字)

  • The character 亏 is a pictograph. Its ancient form depicted a puff of breath or vital energy (气) being exhaled from a person's mouth, or an empty vessel. This exhalation or emptiness represents a loss of energy or substance. Therefore, the character's original and core meaning is 'deficiency', 'shortage', or 'loss'.

The Semantic Evolution:

  • Its most direct meaning is "to suffer a loss," especially in a financial or social context. This is seen in the very common phrases 亏本 (kuīběn), "to lose money in business," and 吃亏 (chīkuī), "to be at a disadvantage." A company's financial loss is a 亏损 (kuīsǔn).
  • From the idea of causing a loss to others, it also means "to treat unfairly" or "to let down." For example, 我不能亏待你 (Wǒ bùnéng kuīdài nǐ) means "I can't treat you unfairly." A guilty conscience is a 心亏 (xīnkuī).
  • Special Evolution: In a surprising rhetorical shift, it developed an adverbial meaning of "fortunately" or "thanks to." This evolved from the polite expression of "being at a loss without" someone's help (e.g., "Thanks to you, I was not at a loss"). It is often seen as 幸亏 (xìngkuī).
  • For example, 幸亏你来了，不然我们就输了。 (Xìngkuī nǐ lái le, bùrán wǒmen jiù shū le), "Fortunately you came, otherwise we would have lost."
  • Loss of breath (Pictograph) → Deficiency / Loss (Original meaning) → To treat unfairly (To cause a loss) → Fortunately / Thanks to (Rhetorical shift).
